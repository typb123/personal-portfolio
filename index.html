About Me

I'm currently a junior at the University of Oregon, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and Computer Science, with an anticipated graduation date of June 2026. My journey into technology began with an innate curiosity for how things work. During the coronavirus pandemic and initially through YouTube, I discovered a deep fascination for physics and the universe. Inspired by renowned astrophysicists like Stephen Hawking, Brian Cox, Brian Greene, and others I continue to explore these concepts through reading and independent study.

Though tempted to major in physics, I ultimately chose to continue to channel my passion into mathematics and computer science. I believe that my technical education can seamlessly blend with my personal interests. Professionally, I am particularly drawn to backend software engineering, excited by the possibilities of creating robust and scalable systems. My interest areas—such as space-based technologies, artificial intelligence, and machine learning—reflect a personal enthusiasm, but I remain versatile and eager to contribute my skills across various domains within computer science.

Before attending the University of Oregon, I completed my general education at Portland Community College while simultaneously working full-time in the kitchen of a retirement home. These experiences in the food industry profoundly shaped my professional character. I developed strong customer service abilities through direct resident interaction, honed my capacity to collaborate effectively within diverse teams, and cultivated resilience and adaptability in dynamic, high-pressure environments.

Today, I'm ready to integrate these invaluable soft skills with my technical knowledge to excel in a technical role. Whether it's developing innovative backend solutions, contributing to groundbreaking space exploration projects, or advancing AI capabilities, I am committed to leveraging my unique blend of experiences to make meaningful contributions.
